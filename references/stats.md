## Industry benchmarks (cited)

### Food cost percentage

- **Healthy full-service:** 28–32% (Toast, R365, NRA *Uniform System of Accounts for Restaurants*)
- **QSR / fast-casual:** 25–30%
- **Pizza concepts:** 22–28%
- **Fine dining:** 30–35%
- **Best-in-class operators target:** under 28% prime / weekly tracking
- **Industry benchmark for "in trouble":** sustained above 36% full-service

### Labor cost percentage (concept-aware)

- **QSR:** 20–25% labor cost
- **Fast casual:** 25–30%
- **Casual dining:** 28–35%
- **Fine dining:** 30–40%
- **Pizza:** 25–30%
- **Coffee / cafe:** 25–32%
- Sources: Toast labor benchmarks, 7shifts, R365

### SPLH (Sales Per Labor Hour)

- **QSR:** $40–60/hr
- **Fast casual:** $55–80/hr
- **Casual dining:** $60–85/hr
- **Fine dining:** $80–150/hr
- **Pizza:** $50–80/hr
- Sources: 7shifts, R365

### Liquor pour cost

- **Liquor / spirits:** 18–24%
- **Beer (draft):** 18–24%
- **Beer (bottled):** 25–30%
- **Wine (by glass):** 28–32%
- **Wine (by bottle):** 30–40%
- **Combined beverage program:** 20–25%
- Sources: Toast, BinWise, BevSpot, USBG

### Inventory variance

- **Best-in-class:** under 1% of sales
- **Acceptable / normal range:** 2–3% of sales
- **Investigate:** above 3%
- **Audit immediately / red flag:** above 5%
- Sources: MarginEdge, R365, BinWise, AICPA *Audit & Accounting Guide: Gaming and Hospitality*

### Pour spec / volume

- **US standard cocktail pour:** 1.5 oz
- **Shot:** 1 oz
- **Heavy pour:** 2 oz
- **Wine BTG (by-the-glass):** 5 oz
- **Standard 750ml bottle:** 25.36 fl oz
- **1L bottle:** 33.81 fl oz
- **1.75L (handle):** 59.17 fl oz
- **Industry-standard spillage:** 5%
- Sources: USBG, Toast pour cost guides, BevSpot

### Tip pool / FLSA

- **Federal tipped minimum cash wage:** $2.13/hour
- **Federal regular minimum:** $7.25/hour
- **Tip credit gap:** $5.12/hour (employer can claim if tipped employees reach $7.25 with tips)
- **No-tip-credit states:** California, Washington, Oregon, Nevada, Montana, Minnesota, Alaska
- **CAA 2018:** managers/supervisors prohibited from receiving pooled tips (FLSA §3(m)(2)(B))
- **2021 DOL final rule:** BOH inclusion in pool allowed only when no tip credit taken
- **August 2024 *Restaurant Law Center v. DOL* (5th Cir.):** vacated the 80/20/30 rule
- **Typical FLSA back-wage settlement:** six figures + liquidated damages = effectively double back wages

### Prime cost (food + labor combined)

- **Healthy:** 55–60% (NRA target)
- **Watch:** 60–65%
- **Critical:** above 65%

### Burden / fully-loaded labor

- **Industry rule of thumb multiplier:** wages × 1.20–1.30
- **FICA (employer side):** 7.65% (Social Security 6.2% + Medicare 1.45%)
- **FUTA effective:** ~0.6% (after credit, on first $7K)
- **Workers comp restaurant class (NCCI 9082/9083):** 1.5–4% of payroll, varies by state
- Sources: IRS Pub 15, Restaurant365, 7shifts, Sling

### Charm pricing

- Round to **nearest .95** (not always up — $15.00 → $14.95, $15.50 → $15.95)
- Round to **nearest .99** for QSR / value pricing

### Q-factor (recipe miscellaneous absorption)

- **Industry standard:** 5–10% added to plate cost for bread, garnish, condiments, packaging, sub-recipe rounding
- Sources: Toast, Culinary Pro

### Comp / waste budget

- **Healthy budget:** 3–5% of food sales
- $80,000/month food sales × 5% = $4,000/month comp budget

## What to never invent

- **Specific dollar amounts attached to a named operator** (Cosmos, Felix's Flaco's, Joe's Manolos) — Brock has not given exact numbers, will not put them in his mouth
- **Specific dates attached to a named incident** unless verified
- **Specific revenue/profit figures for any real business**
